Where to Start with Star Wars

June 9, 2026

Ask ten fans how to watch Star Wars and you will get eleven answers. Release order (IV, V, VI, I, II, III, VII...) preserves the original surprises — especially the father reveal. Chronological order (I through IX) tells Anakin's story as one continuous arc from slave boy to Sith Lord to redeemed spirit.

For newcomers, I usually recommend starting with A New Hope, The Empire Strikes Back, and Return of the Jedi. Those three films are the heart of everything. Once you love those, go back for the prequels, then dive into the sequels and spin-offs on your own schedule.

There is no wrong entry point if you actually finish what you start. Rogue One works as a standalone war movie. Andor is brilliant even if you have never seen a single lightsaber duel. Pick a door and walk through it — the galaxy will still be there when you are ready for more.
