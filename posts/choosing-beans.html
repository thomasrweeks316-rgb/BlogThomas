Choosing the Right Beans

Green coffee is the raw material for everything that follows. Origin tells you a lot about what to expect: African beans often bring floral and citrus notes, while Central American lots tend toward chocolate and nuttiness.

Processing matters too. Washed coffees are clean and bright. Natural-processed beans can be fruitier and more intense. Honey-processed lots often sit somewhere in between.

Freshness counts. Look for green coffee that has been stored well and roasted within a few months of arrival. Old beans roast unevenly and rarely reward the effort.
