Equipment Guide for Beginners

You do not need a commercial drum roaster to get started. Many home roasters begin with a modified popcorn popper or a simple stovetop setup. The goal is consistent heat and enough airflow to keep the beans moving.

As you progress, dedicated home roasters offer better control over temperature curves and batch size. Look for machines with clear visibility, reliable cooling, and easy chaff collection.

Whatever you use, a good scale, a timer, and a notebook will take you further than any piece of gear alone. Track your roasts, taste the results, and adjust from there.
