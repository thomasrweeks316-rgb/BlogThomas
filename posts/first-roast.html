My First Roast

The beans went in green and came out somewhere between "interesting" and "charcoal." That first batch taught me more about heat, airflow, and patience than any tutorial ever could.

I started with a small batch of Ethiopian Yirgacheffe — probably not the easiest choice for a beginner, but the floral notes I was chasing made every second at the roaster worth it.

First crack hit earlier than I expected. I pulled the batch a little late, but the cup still had bright acidity and a sweetness that convinced me to keep going.
