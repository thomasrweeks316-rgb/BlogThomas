Understanding Roast Levels

Roast level is not just a color — it is a balance between origin character and roast development. Pull too early and you get grassy, underdeveloped flavors. Go too far and origin nuance disappears behind smoke and bitterness.

Light roasts preserve acidity and fruit-forward notes. Medium roasts bring more body and caramel sweetness. Dark roasts emphasize chocolate, smoke, and a heavier mouthfeel.

The best roast level is the one that highlights what you love about a particular bean. There is no single correct answer — only what tastes right in your cup.
