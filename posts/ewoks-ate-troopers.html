The Ewoks Ate the Stormtroopers

June 11, 2026

Return of the Jedi ends with a joyful celebration on Endor — fireworks, dancing Ewoks, hugs all around. It is one of the happiest moments in the saga. But look closer at what happened on that forest moon before the party started, and the theory writes itself.

Early in the film, the Ewoks capture Luke, Han, and Chewie and prepare to cook them for a feast. C-3PO barely talks them out of it. These are not cuddly teddy bears — they are apex predators who saw three humans and immediately started building a rotisserie. That is canon. That happened on screen.

By the end of the battle, the Ewoks have defeated an entire legion of Imperial stormtroopers using traps, rocks, and sheer tribal ferocity. Bodies are scattered across the forest floor. The Ewoks are shown dragging helmets around and celebrating over fallen armor. Now ask the uncomfortable question: what does a culture that eats prisoners do with dozens of dead enemies and no refrigeration on a remote moon?

The victory bonfire in the original celebration scene takes on a very different tone once you consider it. Fans have joked for years that the Ewoks' post-battle feast was not just roasted gaffi stick meat. Lucasfilm will never confirm this, and the Disney era has leaned hard into cute Ewok merchandising. But the evidence was there all along, hiding behind a drum and a smile.
